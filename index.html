Summarize the lesson about “The Self from the Psychological Perspective”

William James' Self Theory indicates that human beings individual perception can be divided into two, first is the capacity to be a thinking subject called the ‘I’, and second to be the object of one self’s thinking at the same time called the ‘Me’.

Carl Rogers’ Self Theory indicates that there is a ‘Real Self’ which is the one the person most identifies with, and an ‘Ideal Self’ the person who we want to be, and in order to have ‘Congruence’ the ideal self should be closer to the real self.

The Global Model is the principle that we should look at a person’s totality rather being broken down into parts. Differentiated Model in the other hand, is examining a person through its parts for it can be divisible or broken down into components.

Albert Bandura’s Agentic Theory of the Self points out that selfhood is not culturally influence or controlled by urges, rather every human has the capability of thinking, controlling one’s action, and is free to decide by him or herself.
